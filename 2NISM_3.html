Reflections

Unit 1

    The first learning discussion case study (Glisson, 2015) shows how could cyber threats attack medical devices. With the series of attacks, brute force attacks and denial of service attacks (DoS), the WiFi security protocols vulnerability could be exploited. It had reflected that the old version of the technology could be dangerous while no regular update. I had reviewed the weak password and the complexity rules set up before, changing with the stronger passwords with more combinations. Moreover, I did some research about BackTrack 5, which is the old version of Kali Linux since 2013 (Backtrack, 2013).
    From the lecture cast, gained some knowledge of network history and definitions and the theory of security.

    References:
    Backtrack. (March 13, 2013) Available: https://www.backtrack-linux.org/ [Accessed 13 November 2021].
    Glisson, W., Andel, T., McDonald, T., Jacobs, M., Campbell, M. & Mayr, J. (2015) Compromising a Medical Mannequin. Healthcare Information Systems and Technology (Sighealth). Available: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/281487935_Compromising_a_Medical_Mannequin [Accessed 13 November 2021].

Unit 2

    After the team assignment launched, I created a new Signal group and contact every member to join the group. Although there are only 3 members joining, we started to work on seminar 1 preparation. Each of us provided the DREAD rating table for the presentation, it is a great tool for quantifying the cyber risk, we might consider using it again.
    From the peer response, I did research on the vulnerability of WPA2 and WPA3 including Key Reinstallation Attacks (KRACK) and Dragonblood attacks (Mathy & Eyal, 2019). That surprised me about the weakness in the security of WiFi. I soon had reviewed the WiFi router which I set up, upgraded firmware and changed the security to WPA3 instead of WPA2. In addition, I did research on Zero Trust (ZT) technology which could consider for the next business plan of my work.

    References:
    Mathy, V. & Eyal, R. (April 2019) Analysing WPA3's Dragonfly Handshake. DRAGONBLOOD. Available from: https://wpa3.mathyvanhoef.com/ [Accessed 22 November 2021].

Unit 3

    From the initial post feedback, the access control mentioned by Beran (2021), it could be argued that grant access should be based on the principle of least privilege (PoLP) security concept (Cyberark, 2021). In my experience, it could be a time-consuming part to design those access from an IT point of view. It would be better to involve the business user to discuss their need and let them test them. In addition, adopting Privileged password management (PPM) which generate the password for temporary privileged access could be a solution to control the super admin right.
    For the team activity, we made use of the standard network tools to scan the basic information for our target site. Discuss our findings and understand the variety of the result, decide what we would present in the seminar. Understand that team members might not be able to join every seminar, at this point, I suggested the rotation of the presenter in our team.
    The lecture cast provided a refreshment on the Network Fundamentals with the OSI model.

    References:
    Cyberark (2021) Principle of Least Privilege. Cyberark Glossary. Available from: https://www.cyberark.com/what-is/least-privilege/ [Accessed 27 November 2021].

Unit 4

    Learning discussion 2 requires us to explain the standard network tools findings. Normally I used those tools for network testing or troubleshooting only, however, we used them to analyse a website which requires understanding the mechanism behind those tools and pointing out the important information that might be missed. In addition, after researching the registered information of the whois command, found that information could be registered to the domain name wholesaler company (Enom, N.D.).
    For seminar 2 preparation, I did research and compare it with TCP/IP and ISO/OSI, 7 layers are the common term which explained by the network team for different level issues during my work, however, practically we used TCP/IP.

    References:
    Enom (N.D.) About Enom. About. Available from: https://www.enom.com/reseller/about-enom/ [Accessed 4 December 2021].

Unit 5

    During this week, I installed the Kali Linux on the virtual machine for evaluation, it comes with a lot of powerful tools, it would require researching the guide and command sample on how to use those.
    In the peer response of learning discussion 2, found that Nmap is a slim but powerful tool to detect port and software versions. The version could be detected by common services associated ports and response from the port to gather data. For security concerns, it could be better to set the customize or by using Network Address Port Translation (NAPT) to redirect a different port number to the internal service (Parziale et al, 2006).
    Moreover, found the target site has been configured Sender Policy Framework (SPF) with text strings recorded in the Domain Name System (DNS) (Parziale et al, 2006). The SPF record is a basic way to prevent domain spoofing (Wong & Schlitt, 2006) which I usually set for the mail server before.

    References:
    Nmap (N.D.) Port Specification and Scan Order. Nmap Reference Guide. Available from: https://nmap.org/book/man-port-specification.html [Accessed 10 December 2021].
    Parziale, L. et al, (2006) TCP/IP tutorial & technical overview. IBM Redbooks. Available via the Vitalsource Bookshelf [Accessed 10 December 2021].
    Wong, M. & Schlitt, W. (April 2006) Sender Policy Framework (SPF) for Authorizing Use of Domains in E-Mail. Experimental. Available from: https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/html/rfc4408 [Accessed 10 December 2021].

Unit 6

    In the summary post, I could finally explain why the traceroute result could be empty for Internet Control Message Protocol (ICMP). The exploitability of ICMP echo requests could be a cyber threat that should be filtered. There are easy and effective to block those requests now a day such as a web application firewall (WAF).
    During my penetration test with Kali Linux, WAF could block quite a lot of attacks, it had blocked my IP addresses more than 20 times. Even I tried to use the proxy server, the false results were returned during the test. I may try to think of other ways for a successful and accurate penetration result.
    For the team activity evaluation exercise, Geer (2015) blog post provide 8 penetration tools to compare which provided a useful suggestion for choosing the tool. For all the tools which I have already installed and tested in Kali Linux, I have provided the initial rating table and demonstrated each tool to my team member. Moreover, I was assigned to write the security vulnerabilities and review the recommendation for the Team Project Design Document. Due to the page and word limit, I decide to use the DREAD table to combine vulnerabilities and recommendations. In addition, categorize and group the items to have a clear and summarized presentation.

    References:
    Geer, D. (2015) 8 Penetration Testing Tools That Will Do the Job. (Network World) [Accessed 4 December 2021].
