    Unit 1

    Detailed information regarding the Codes of Ethics and Professional Conduct was supplied at the start of this module. The Association of Computing Machinery (ACM) has produced several case studies that would be discussed. The "Reasoning Quiz" used a few examples to assess the comprehension of inductive and deductive reasoning.
    Understanding the British Computer Society's (BCS) Code of Conduct, which outlines the qualities of IT professionals, demonstrates the seriousness with which the development of a morally upright and responsible computer industry is taken. Using examples, describe the effects of wrongdoing and its consequences.
    I should constantly keep ethics and conduct in mind as an IT professional. According to the regulations and requirements for daily jobs. The white hat hackers and how to define "good" were also covered in the essay "Ethics vs Morality" (John, 2018). That makes me think about the work I do on a daily basis since there are a lot of grey areas that could be difficult to categorise as right or wrong.

    Unit 4

    'Implementing Cyber Security tools and/or techniques in detecting medical misinformation on a social media platform of Twitter' is the topic I picked for my literature review. Most of the medical material on social media in recent years has been about the information on COVID-19 (Sotto & Viviani, 2022). The fight against disinformation is crucial given the quick and widespread diffusion of information on social media platforms like Twitter, Facebook, and YouTube.
    After doing some investigation, I discovered that several studies have suggested utilising machine learning algorithms to identify false news predictions (Marriboyina et al, 2021). Despite it is not a cyber security tool, it is a reliable method to use as a stage in the process of identifying medical misinformation.
    References:
    Sotto, S. & Viviani, M. (2022) Health Misinformation Detection in the Social Web: An Overview and a Data Science Approach. Int J Environ Res Public Health 19(4):2173. DOI: 10.3390/ijerph19042173
    Marriboyina, S., Gogulamudi, V., Kumar, K., Aruna, B. & Yadav, A. (2021). Prediction of Fake Tweets Using Machine Learning Algorithms. Recent Trends in Intensive Computing DOI: 10.3233/APC210195.
